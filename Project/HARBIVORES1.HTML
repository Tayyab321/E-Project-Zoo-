HARBIVORES

A herbivore is an animal that gets its energy from eating plants, and only plants. Omnivores can also eat parts of plants, but generally only the fruits and vegetables produced by fruit-bearing plants. Many herbivores have special digestive systems that let them digest all kinds of plants, including grasses.

Cattle

Cattle are large, domesticated, cloven-hooved herbivores. They are a prominent modern member of the subfamily Bovinae and the most widespread species of the genus Bos.

Speed: 40 km/h

Family: Bovidae

Subfamily: Bovinae

Mass: 1,100 kg (Adult, Bull), 720 kg (Adult, Cow)

Goat

The goat is a domesticated species of goat-antelope typically kept as livestock. It was domesticated from the wild goat of Southwest Asia and Eastern Europe.

Goat

Scientific name: Capra aegagrus hircus

Eats: grasses, weeds, grain, and sometimes even tree bark

Deer

Deer or true deer are hoofed ruminant mammals forming the family Cervidae.For many types of deer in modern English usage, the male is a buck and the female a doe

Deer

Speed: 50 km/h

Family: Cervidae; Goldfuss, 1820

Class: Mammalia

Order: Artiodactyla

Lifespan: about 2 to 3 years.

Height:85 – 150 cm

Eats:Grasses, sedges, the leaves and shoots of trees

Bite Force: around 1,100 psi

Horse

The horse is a domesticated, odd-toed, hoofed mammal. It belongs to the taxonomic family Equidae and is one of two extant subspecies of Equus ferus.

Gorillas are herbivorous, predominantly ground-dwelling great apes that inhabit the tropical forests of equatorial Africa.
