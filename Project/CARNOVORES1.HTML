CARNIVORES

A carnivore is an organism that mostly eats meat, or the flesh of animals. Sometimes carnivores are called predators. Organisms that carnivores hunt are called prey. Carnivores are a major part of the food web, a description of which organisms eat which other organisms in the wild.

Tiger

The tiger is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. An apex predator, it primarily preys on ungulates such as deer and wild boar.

Tiger

Scientific name: Panthera tigris

Height: 80 – 85 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

Mass:90 – 310 kg (Adult), 65 – 180 kg (Adult)

Speed:49 – 65 km/h (Adult, In Short Bursts)

Kingdom:Animalia

Family:Felidae

Lifespan 8 – 10 years (In the wild)

Bite Force:1,050 psi

Lion

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail.

Lion

Speed: 80 km/h (Maximum, In Short Bursts)

Family: Felidae

Scientific name: Panthera leo

Subfamily: Pantherinae

Lifespan: 10 – 15 years (Adult, In the wild), 8 –
10 years (Adult, In the wild)

Mass: 190 kg (Adult), 130 kg (Adult)

Bite Force: 650 PSI

Wolf

The wolf, also known as the gray wolf or grey wolf, is a large canine native to Eurasia and North America.

Wolf

Scientific name: Canis lupus/p>

Eats: Arctic fox

Height: 80 – 85 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

Mass: 30 – 80 kg (Adult), 23 – 55 kg (Adult)

Speed:70 km/h

Kingdom:Animalia

Family:Canidae

Bite Force:almost 400 pounds of pressure per square inch!

Hyenas

Hyenas, or hyaenas, are feliform carnivoran mammals of the family Hyaenidae. With only four extant species, it is the fifth-smallest biological family in the Carnivora and one of the smallest in the class Mammalia.

Hyenas

Speed: 64 km/h

Family: Hyaenidae; Gray, 1821

Class: Mammalia

Order: Carnivora

Suborder:Feliformia

Height:Spotted hyena: 70 – 92 cm, Striped hyena: 60 – 80 cm, Brown hyena: 70 – 80 cm

Bite Force: around 1,100 psi

Bear

Bears are carnivoran mammals of the family Ursidae.They are classified as caniforms, or doglike carnivorans.

Bear

Scientific Name:Ursidae

Family: Ursidae; G. Fischer de Waldheim, 1817

Class: Mammalia

Kingdom: Animalia

Order: Carnivora

phylum: Chordata

Speed: Polar bear: 40 km/h, Brown bear: 56 km/h

Height:70 – 150 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

Bite Force: 975 psi

Cheetah

The cheetah is a large cat native to Africa and central Iran. It is the fastest land animal, estimated to be capable of running at 80 to 128 km/h with the fastest reliably recorded speeds being 93 and 98 km/h.

cheetah

Speed: 93 and 98 km/h

Family: Felidae

Scientific name: Acinonyx jubatus

Subfamily: Felinae

Mass: 21 – 72 kg (Adult)

Bite Force: 400-500PSI

Height 67 – 94 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

African Wild Dog

The African wild dog also called the African painted dog and the African hunting dog, is a wild canine which is a native species to sub-Saharan Africa.

African Wild Dog

Speed: more than 44 miles per hour

Family: Canidae

Scientific name: Lycaon pictus

Subfamily: Caninae

Lifespan: about 10 years

Mass: 22 kg (Adult)

Bite Force: 317 PSI

Genus: lycoan

Wolverine

The wolverine, Gulo gulo, also referred to as the glutton, carcajou, or quickhatch, is the largest land-dwelling species of the family Mustelidae. It is a muscular carnivore and a solitary animal.

Wolverine

Scientific name:Gulo gulo

Length:65 – 110 cm (Adult)

Mass:9 – 25 kg (Adult)

Speed:up to 30 miles per hour

Kingdom: Animalia

Family:Mustelidae

Bite Force:50 PSI

Lifespan: 5 – 13 years (In the wild)
