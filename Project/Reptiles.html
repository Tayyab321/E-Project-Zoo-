Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates. (Vertebrates have backbones.) They have dry skin covered with scales or bony plates and usually lay soft-shelled eggs.

Famous Reptiles

American Alligator

The American alligator, sometimes referred to colloquially as a gator or common alligator, is a large crocodilian reptile native to the Southeastern United States

American Alligator

Common Name: American Alligator

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Alligator mississippiensis

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

GROUP NAME: Congregation

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 35 to 50 years

SIZE: 10 to 15 feet

WEIGHT: 1,000 pounds

Desert Tortoise

Desert tortoises live in the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Desert Tortoise

Common Name: Desert Tortoise

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Gopherus agassizii

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

Group: Testudinae family

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 30 to 50 years

SIZE: 14 inches in Length

WEIGHT: 0.02 to 5 kg

Mexican Beaded Lizard

The Mexican beaded lizard is a species of lizard in the family Helodermatidae, found principally in Mexico and southern Guatemala .

Mexican Beaded Lizard

Common Name: Mexican Beaded Lizard

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Heloderma horridum

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

Family: Helodermatidae

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 20 to 40 years

SIZE: 3 Feet in Length

WEIGHT: 1.4 to 2 kilograms

Softshell Turtles

Softshells include some of the world's largest freshwater turtles, though many can adapt to living in highly brackish areas

Softshell Turtles

Common Name: Softshell Turtles

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Trionychidae

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

GROUP NAME: Chitrainae

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 35 to 50 years

FEMALE SIZE: 7 to 19 inches

MALE SIZE: 5 to 10 inches

WEIGHT: 2.7 to 6.6 kg

Marsh crocodile

The mugger crocodile also known as marsh crocodile.It is native to freshwater habitats from southern Iran to the Indian subcontinent

Marsh crocodile

Common Name: Marsh crocodile

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Crocodylus palustris

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: LIFE SPAN: 20 to 30 years

SIZE: 3.2 m Length

WEIGHT: 40 to 200 kg

Chameleon

Chameleons mostly live in the rain forests and deserts of Africa . The color of their skin helps them blend in with their habitats.Chameleons that hang out in trees are green

Chameleon

Common Name: Meller's Chameleon

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Trioceros melleri

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

GROUP NAME: Congregation

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 12 years

SIZE: 21 inches

WEIGHT: 14.4 ounces

Bosc's fringe-toed lizard

Bosc's fringe-toed lizard is a species of lizard in the family Lacertidae. The species is endemic to North Africa and Western Asia. They are also fast moving lizard.

Bosc's fringe-toed lizard

Common Name: Bosc's fringe-toed lizard

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Acanthodactylus boskianus

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

FAMILY: Lacertidae

SIZE: 5 and 8 cm

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Leatherbacks are one of the more endangered creatures on Earth. They are often caught by accident in fishing nets. Stuck underwater, they drown

Leatherback Sea Turtle

COMMON NAME: Leatherback Sea Turtle

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Dermochelys coriacea

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 45 years

SIZE: Up to 7 feet

WEIGHT: Up to 2,000 pounds

Pancake Tortoise

The pancake tortoise is a species of flat-shelled tortoise in the family Testudinidae. The species is native to Tanzania and Kenya .

Pancake Tortoise

Common Name: Pancake Tortoise

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Malacochersus tornieri

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: More than 30 years

SIZE: 7 inches

WEIGHT: 1 pound

Green Sea Turtle

Green sea turtles spend most of their lives underwater, where they can rest for up to five hours at a time before coming up for air.

Green Sea Turtle

COMMON NAME: Green Sea Turtle

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Chelonia myda

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Herbivore

GROUP NAME: Bale

SIZE: Up to 5 feet

WEIGHT: Up to 700 pounds

Blue-tongued lizards

Blue-tongued skinks comprise the Australasian genus Tiliqua, which contains some of the largest members of the skink family.

Blue-tongued lizards

Common Name: Blue-tongued lizards

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Tiliqua

TYPE: Reptiles

DIET: Carnivore

AVERAGE LIFE SPAN IN THE WILD: 20 years

SIZE: Length( 30 – 60 cm (Adult))

WEIGHT: 490 g
