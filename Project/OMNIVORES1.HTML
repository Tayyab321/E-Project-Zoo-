OMNIVORES

An omnivore is an animal that has the ability to eat and survive on both plant and animal matter. Obtaining energy and nutrients from plant and animal matter, omnivores digest carbohydrates, protein, fat, and fiber, and metabolize the nutrients and energy of the sources absorbed.

Chimpanzee

The chimpanzee, also known simply as chimp, is a species of great ape native to the forest and savannah of tropical Africa. It has four confirmed subspecies and a fifth proposed subspecies.

Chimpanzee

Scientific name: Canis lupus/p>

Speed: 25mph (40kph)

Family: Hominidae

Scientific name:Pan troglodytes

Subfamily: Homininae

OrderPrimates

Lifespan: 39 years (In captivity), 32 years (In captivity)

Mass:40 – 60 kg (Adult), 32 – 47 kg (Adult)

Badger

Badgers are short-legged omnivores in the family Mustelidae. Badgers are a polyphyletic grouping and are not a natural taxonomic grouping.

Bedger

Scientific name: Meles meles

Eats: Earthworms, frogs, rodents, birds, eggs, lizards, insects, bulbs, seeds and berries.

Length:71 cm (Adult)

Mass: 12 kg (Adult)

Speed:16-19 mph (25–30 kmph)

Kingdom:Animalia

Family:Mustelidae Mephitidae

Lifespan: 24 years

Civet

A civet is a small, lean, mostly nocturnal mammal native to tropical Asia and Africa, especially the tropical forests. The term civet applies to over a dozen different species, mostly from the family Viverridae.

Civet

Scientific Name:Civettictis civetta

Speed: 50 km/h

Family: Cervidae; Goldfuss, 1820

Class: Mammalia

Mass:7 – 20 kg (Adult)

Lifespan: 15 – 20 years

Height:40 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

Eats: fruits of the Fishtail Palm and seed pods of the Rain Tree

Hedgehog

A hedgehog is a spiny mammal of the subfamily Erinaceinae, in the eulipotyphlan family Erinaceidae.

Hedgehog

Scientific Name:Erinaceinae

Family: Erinaceidae

Kingdom: Animalia

Order: Eulipotyphla

Speed:4 miles per hour

Lifespan: 2 – 5 year

Height:4 to 12 inches

Length:24 cm (Adult)

Eats:worms, beetles, slugs, caterpillars, earwigs and millipedes

Fox

Foxes are small to medium-sized, omnivorous mammals belonging to several genera of the family Canidae.

Fox

Speed: Red fox: 50 km/h, Gray fox: 68 km/h

Family: Canidae

Mass: 2.2 – 14 kg (Adult)

Scientific name:Vulpes vulpes

Eats: small mammals, birds, eggs, insects, fish, crabs, worms, reptiles, mollusks, fruits

Lifespan: 3 – 4 years (In the wild)

HeightFennec fox: 20 cm, Arctic fox: 25 – 30 cm, Red fox: 35 – 50 cm

Pig

The pig, often called swine, hog, or domestic pig when distinguishing from ot her members of the genus Sus, is an omnivorous, domesticated, even-toed, hoofed mammal.

Pig

Scientific name: : Sus scrofa domesticus

Height: Bentheim Black Pied pig: 75 cm, Angeln Saddleback: 92 cm

Mass:between 50 and 350 kg

Speed:40 km/h

Family:Suidae

Kingdom:animalia

Lifespan 15 – 20 years

Squirrel

Squirrels are members of the family Sciuridae, a family that includes small or medium-size rodents. The squirrel family includes tree squirrels, ground squirrels, and flying squirrels.

Squirrel

Speed: 32 km/h

Family: Sciuridae

Scientific name: Sciuridae

Class: Mammalia

Eats: seeds, nuts and fruits, but they will also munch on eggs, small insects

Lifespan:Alpine marmot: 15 – 18 years, Siberian chipmunk: 6 – 10 years

Mass: about 1 to 1.5 pounds

Tapanuli Oranguntan

The Tapanuli orangutan is a species of orangutan restricted to South Tapanuli in the island of Sumatra in Indonesia.

Tapanuli Oranguntan

Scientific name: Pongo tapanuliensis

Height: females under 4 feet (1.21 m) in height and males under 5 feet (1.53 m).

Mass:70–90 kg (150–200 lb)

Speed: 2.7 mph

Kingdom: Animalia

Family: Hominidae

Lifespan: 35-40 years in the wild

Gaint Panda

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear, is a bear species endemic to China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.

Gaint Panda

Scientific Name:Ailuropoda melanoleuca

Family: Ursidae

kingdom: Animalia

Order: Carnivora

Speed: 56 – 69 km/h (Adult, In Pursuit)

Height:60 – 90 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)

Eats:almost entirely on bamboo

Mass 70 – 120 kg (Adult)
